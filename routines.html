Pull-Up Progression Guide

Pull-ups are one of the best upper body exercises for building strength and muscle. This progression will take you from zero to 20+ pull-ups.

Beginner Level

  • Dead Hangs: 3 sets of 20-30 seconds
  • Scapular Pulls: 3 sets of 5-8 reps
  • Negative Pull-ups: 3 sets of 3-5 reps (jump up, lower slowly)
  • Band-Assisted Pull-ups: 3 sets of 5-8 reps

Intermediate Level

  • Scapular Pulls: 2 sets of 8-10 reps
  • Chin-ups: 3 sets of 3-5 reps
  • Pull-ups: 3 sets of 3-5 reps
  • Negative Pull-ups: 2 sets of 5 reps (3-second lowering phase)

Advanced Level

  • Pull-ups: 5 sets of 5-8 reps
  • Pull-up Variations: Wide grip, close grip, L-sit
  • Weighted Pull-ups: Add weight when you can do 12+ regular pull-ups
  • Pull-up Ladders: 1 rep, rest, 2 reps, rest, 3 reps, etc. until failure

Implementation Notes

  • Rest 90-120 seconds between sets
  • Focus on full range of motion
  • Keep shoulders engaged/active throughout movement
  • Progress to next level when you can complete all prescribed reps/sets

Handstand & Press Handstand Guide

Handstands develop impressive upper body strength, balance, and body awareness. This progression will take you from wall supports to freestanding handstands and presses.

Handstand Progression

  • Wall Plank: Face wall, hands 6" from wall, hold 30-60 seconds Ã— 3 sets
  • Wall Walks: Walk feet up wall to increasing heights, 5-8 reps
  • Wall Handstand: Belly facing wall, 3 sets of 30-60 seconds
  • Toe Taps: From wall handstand, alternate taking one foot off wall, 5-8 reps per foot
  • Freestanding Handstand: Start with 5-10 second holds, gradually increase

Press Handstand Progression

  • Pike Press: From downward dog, press shoulders forward, 8-10 reps
  • Elevated Pike Press: Feet on box/bench, press shoulders forward, 6-8 reps
  • Tuck Press: From squat, lift hips up and over shoulders, 5-8 reps
  • Straddle Press: From straddle forward fold, press up, 3-5 reps
  • Full Press: From standing, fold forward and press to handstand

Wrist Preparation (Essential)

  • Wrist circles: 10 in each direction
  • Finger pulses: 10 reps
  • Wrist stretches: 20-30 seconds each position

Splits & Flexibility Training

Developing flexibility requires consistent practice and proper technique. This guide will help you progress safely toward full splits.

Front Splits Routine

  • Hip Flexor Stretch: Low lunge with back knee down, 60 seconds per side
  • Hamstring Stretch: Forward fold with straight legs, 60 seconds
  • Half Split: Front leg straight, back leg bent, 60 seconds per side
  • PNF for Front Split: In deepest split position, contract muscles for 10 seconds, relax deeper for 30 seconds, repeat 3 times

Side Splits Routine

  • Adductor Stretch: Seated butterfly, 60 seconds
  • Cossack Squat: Side lunge with one leg straight, alternate sides, 10 reps each
  • Frog Pose: Knees wide on floor, 60 seconds
  • PNF for Side Split: In deepest straddle, press legs down for 10 seconds, relax deeper for 30 seconds, repeat 3 times

General Tips

  • Always warm up before stretching (5 minutes of light cardio)
  • Use deep breathing to enhance stretches
  • Progress is slow and steady - expect weeks to months of consistent work
  • Slight discomfort is normal, pain is not

Jump Rope Skill Progression

Jump rope training improves coordination, footwork, and cardiovascular endurance while burning calories efficiently.

Beginner Skills

  • Basic Bounce: Both feet together, 1-2 minutes continuous
  • Alternate Step: Walking/jogging in place, 1-2 minutes
  • Side-to-Side: Shifting weight left to right, 30-60 seconds
  • Front-to-Back: Shifting weight forward and back, 30-60 seconds

Intermediate Skills

  • High Knees: Alternating high knees, 30-60 seconds
  • Heel Taps: Alternating heel taps forward, 30-60 seconds
  • Double Unders: Rope passes twice per jump, 5-10 consecutive
  • Crossovers: Cross arms in front of body, 5-10 consecutive

Advanced Skills

  • Triple Unders: Three passes per jump
  • Criss-Cross Combinations: Alternating crossovers
  • Side Swings to Cross: Side swing to crossover
  • Freestyle Combinations: Create your own combinations

Practice Structure

  • Warm up with basic bounce (1 minute)
  • Practice new skills (3-5 minutes)
  • Speed intervals (30 seconds fast, 30 seconds slow Ã— 3)

Juggling Five Balls Progression

Juggling develops hand-eye coordination, focus, and neural pathways. This step-by-step progression will take you from zero to five balls.

Three-Ball Mastery (Required Foundation)

  • Basic Cascade: Until completely comfortable and automatic
  • Reverse Cascade: Inside throws instead of outside
  • Columns: Balls go straight up in columns
  • Tennis: Two in one hand, one in other hand

Four-Ball Progression

  • Four-Ball Fountain: Two in each hand, throw/catch same hand
  • Async Fountain: Right hand slightly ahead of left
  • Four-Ball Wimpy: Like cascade but incomplete pattern
  • Practice Goal: 25+ catches consistently

Five-Ball Progression

  • Five-Ball Flash: Throw all five, catch as many as possible
  • Five-Ball Cascade Start: Focus on first 5-7 throws
  • Partial Patterns: Work up to 10, 15, then 20+ catches
  • Full Pattern: Sustained five-ball cascade

Practice Tips

  • Short, frequent sessions (5 minutes daily)
  • Focus on quality throws, not just catches
  • Record yourself to analyze pattern
  • Practice over a bed to save time picking up dropped balls
